<p><p>Automated Test Equipment (ATE) is categorized into three main types: Non-Memory ATE, Memory ATE, and Discrete ATE. Non-Memory ATE focuses on testing logic and mixed-signal devices without memory components, optimizing performance and reliability. Memory ATE specializes in testing memory chips, ensuring their functionality and speed, critical for modern electronics. Discrete ATE targets individual electronic components, such as resistors and capacitors, assessing their quality and performance. Each type serves distinct sectors within the electronic manufacturing industry.</p></p>
